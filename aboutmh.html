Definition of Mental Health:

Mental health is a state of emotional, psychological, and social well-being affecting how we feel, think, and act. These can be how we cope up with the stresses of our life, realize our abilities, learn and work well with others and ourselves, and contribute to our community. It is very complex, and varies from one person to another.

The Importance of Mental Health:

According to Miles (2025), “...the importance of mental health doesn’t wax and wane throughout our lifetime. At all stages of life, our mental health is essential.” The importance of being mentally healthy is really important as it determines how we perform our everyday activities, it affects every area of our life. Whether you’re a child playing games with friends, a teen knowing more about yourself, a college student studying hard, an adult working in an office, a parent, or a grandparent, mental health should be put first, as everything else follows.

As A Child:

Good mental health in childhood helps kids manage emotions, build confidence, and form healthy friendships. It supports learning, curiosity, and reaching developmental milestones. When children are mentally healthy, they develop stronger coping skills for the future. Childhood is the foundation.

As A Teen:

For teens, mental health is crucial as they face rapid physical and emotional changes. It helps them build identity, make healthy decisions, and handle school and social pressures. Good mental well-being protects them from stress and supports positive relationships.

As An Adult:

Adults with good mental health manage responsibilities, relationships, and work stress more effectively. It supports productivity, problem-solving, and emotional balance. This leads to healthier interactions and a more stable daily life. For adults that are parents, being mentally healthy plays a big role on their children or nieces/nephews.

As A Senior:

In older adulthood, good mental health helps seniors stay emotionally steady, socially connected, and mentally active. It allows them to cope with life changes and maintain independence. This supports better overall health and quality of life. For senior adults that are grandparents, being mentally healthy plays a big role on their grandchildren.

What Would Happen If Mental Health Was Disregarded:

But how does mental health really affect our life? Self-esteem, positivity, healthier relationships, better productivity, higher quality of life—sense of clarity, purpose, passion, belongingness, ability to express, increased resiliency, ability to navigate change, uncertainty—planning in the future, decision-making, and the caring of our other healths like physical and emotional all points out to being mentally healthy.
